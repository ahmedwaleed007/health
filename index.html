Core Concepts & Tools for Health Assessment

1) Blood Pressure (BP)

  • Definition: Blood pressure is the force of blood against artery walls. It is recorded as two numbers: systolic and diastolic BP.
  • Normal BP: Below 120/80 mmHg.
  • Importance: High BP can lead to heart disease, stroke, and kidney damage.

2) Body Mass Index (BMI)

  • Definition: A measurement based on weight and height to assess whether a person is overweight, underweight, or at a healthy weight.
  • Normal BMI: Between 18.5 and 24.9.
  • Importance: Helps in identifying risks for diseases like diabetes and cardiovascular diseases.

3) Indian Growth Charts

  • Purpose: Used to monitor children's growth based on height, weight, and head circumference.
  • Application: Important for children under 5 years to track their growth patterns.

1) Pregnancy & Postpartum (Antenatal to 12 months postpartum)

Antenatal Care (ANC)

  • First Visit: Screening for blood pressure, anemia, urine protein, blood group, and blood sugar.
  • Routine Follow-ups: Monitor weight, BP, fetal growth, and check for preeclampsia.
  • Immunizations: Administer Tetanus Toxoid (TT), Iron Folic Acid (IFA) supplements.

Postpartum (6–12 weeks checkup)

  • Physical Health Check: Inspect wound healing, bleeding, and breastfeeding issues.
  • Mental Health: Screen for postpartum depression using EPDS.
  • Contraception & Family Planning: Discuss family planning methods.

2) Newborn: 0–28 Days

Immediate Care

  • Physical Check: Examine skin, palate, and genitalia for abnormalities.
  • Immunizations: Administer BCG, Hepatitis B, and OPV.
  • Feeding: Initiate breastfeeding within the first hour to provide colostrum.

Newborn Screening Tests

  • Critical Congenital Heart Defects (CCHD): Screen with pulse oximetry.
  • Metabolic Screening: Screen for metabolic disorders like hypothyroidism and phenylketonuria.

3) Infants (1–12 months)

Growth Monitoring

  • Height, Weight: Monitor using Indian Growth Charts.
  • Feeding: Exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months, followed by complementary foods.
  • Developmental Milestones: Social smile, rolling over, sitting without support, and babbling.

Immunizations

  • At 6 Weeks: DTP, Hib, Polio, Hepatitis B, Rotavirus.
  • At 9 Months: Measles, Vitamin A supplementation.

Safety

  • Choking Hazards: Ensure no small objects within reach.
  • Safe Sleep: Always place baby on their back to sleep to reduce SIDS risks.

4) Toddlers & Preschoolers (1–5 years)

Growth & Development

  • Height, Weight: Monitor using Indian Growth Charts.
  • Motor Skills: Monitor walking, talking, and engaging in pretend play.
  • Speech Development: Ensure the child uses words and follows simple instructions.

Immunizations

  • Ensure DTP boosters, Polio, and Measles are up to date as per the Indian Immunization Schedule.

Safety & Injury Prevention

  • Prevent Falls: Ensure play areas are safe.
  • Oral Health: Start brushing with fluoride toothpaste.

5) School-Age Children (6–12 years)

Growth Monitoring

  • Height & Weight: Track using Indian Growth Charts.
  • Activity: Encourage at least 60 minutes of physical activity daily.

Immunizations

  • Ensure DTP, MMR, and Polio boosters are administered.

Mental Health

  • School Stress: Screen for stress, anxiety, or bullying.
